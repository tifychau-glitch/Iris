# Pace Morby's A-Z Calendar Framework
## From Chaos to Controlled Calendar — Owners Club

Each principle is a building block of intentional living. Use these when coaching the user or when they ask about a specific concept.

---

**A — Absolutely Start with Non-Negotiables**
Identify the absolute priorities in your life — family time, health, faith, key relationships. These are the things that, if missed regularly, you'd feel like you failed as a person. These go on the calendar *first*, before any work commitments. Everything else builds around them.

**B — Be Prepared to Make Sacrifices**
Real calendar design requires giving things up. Identify what you're willing to compromise and be intentional about it. The mindset shift is crucial: don't just *tolerate* the sacrifice — commit to it and be genuinely happy that you have things to sacrifice. That means you have a full life worth designing.

**C — Close in on Money-Making Activities**
Block out time specifically for activities that directly contribute to your income or business growth. These are your second-highest priority time blocks after non-negotiables. Be specific — "real estate outreach," "content creation," "investor calls" — not vague categories.

**D — Dedicate Time for Reflection and Creation**
Schedule regular time to reflect on your progress and document your journey. This can include creating content (YouTube, Instagram, TikTok, etc.) or simply journaling. Reflection without documentation is wasted growth.

**E — Explore New Skillsets and Ideas**
Reserve time to learn new skills — books, podcasts, courses — and immediately implement what you've learned into your routine. Learning is only valuable when it's applied.

**F — Focus on Weekly Quiet Planning Time**
Pace recommends a minimum of four hours per week dedicated to organizing your calendar, reflecting on your goals, and strategizing. This is protected time — no meetings, no calls. This is where you work *on* your life, not just *in* it.

**G — Grow Your Productivity Monthly (By Removing Ineffective Processes)**
Every month, identify and eliminate activities or processes that no longer serve your goals or productivity. Growth isn't just about adding more — it's about pruning what's dead weight.

**H — Honestly Evaluate Your Meetings and Activities**
Assess whether each recurring meeting or commitment is truly valuable. Adjust your schedule to avoid unproductive commitments. If a meeting doesn't move you toward your goals, question whether it needs to exist.

**I — Intention is the Key**
Ensure that your time is spent meaningfully, with focus on creating unique and intentional experiences. Every block on your calendar should have a reason. Drifting through the week is the opposite of this principle.

**J — Just Ask Yourself: Do I Truly Want This?**
Periodically evaluate if your goals still align with your actions — and whether your calendar reflects your real intentions. Life changes. Goals can change. The calendar has to keep up. Don't keep pursuing something you no longer believe in.

**K — Keep Track of Your Progress and Wins**
Regularly document your achievements and growth to maintain perspective and motivation. This is how you avoid forgetting how far you've come. It fuels the next stretch.

**L — Live and Die by Your Calendar**
Commit to following your calendar strictly, ensuring that every scheduled item serves a purpose. Your calendar isn't a suggestion — it's a promise you made to yourself. Treat it that way.

**M — Manage People and Expectations**
Train those around you — family, team, colleagues — to respect your calendar and time boundaries. If you don't protect your time, no one else will. This requires clear communication and consistent follow-through.

**N — Never Deviate**
Stick to your established non-negotiables unless a true, absolute priority arises. The goal is to make your calendar predictable and reliable. Exceptions should be rare and deliberate — not default.

**O — Organize Daily**
Spend 15 minutes each morning reviewing and adjusting your calendar for the day. This micro-habit is the anchor that keeps everything else on track. It's not about perfection — it's about awareness and intention each morning.

**P — Define Purpose for Each Activity**
Ensure every meeting and time block on your calendar has a clear objective and outcome. If you can't articulate why something is on your calendar, it probably shouldn't be.

**Q — Question Your Beliefs**
Reflect on whether your routines and commitments align with your true goals and values. Don't just run the program — interrogate it. Are you doing things out of habit, obligation, or fear? Or because they genuinely move you forward?

**R — Reject Unplanned Requests**
Say no to last-minute requests that disrupt your schedule. This includes family members, colleagues, friends. Set clear boundaries. If it's not on the calendar, it doesn't get done — or it waits until there's a proper slot for it.

**S — Slowly Build a Week You're Excited About**
Design a schedule that energizes and motivates you. Prioritize activities that align with your goals. The goal isn't just a productive week — it's a week you actually *want* to live. If you're dreading your own calendar, something is wrong.

**T — Train Yourself and Others to Plan Ahead**
Cultivate the habit of thinking and planning 6-12 months ahead, and encourage those around you to do the same. Big goals require long runways. If you're only planning a week out, you're always reactive.

**U — Understand the Importance of Planning**
Acknowledge that planning is the foundation for achieving both personal and professional success. This isn't a nice-to-have — it's the mechanism that separates people who achieve their goals from those who don't.

**V — Value and Rate Activities**
Evaluate the value of each activity and meeting — does it align with your objectives? If something didn't turn out the way you expected, document what you need to change. Treat your calendar like a portfolio — track your return on time.

**W — Without a Calendar, Don't Blame Others**
Recognize that a lack of a calendar leads to inefficiency and missed opportunities. If your life feels chaotic, scattered, or out of control — the calendar is the solution. Not motivation. Not discipline. The calendar.

**X — Incorporate X-Factors**
Add new relationships, ideas, or opportunities to your schedule to promote growth and innovation. Ask yourself: what is the X-factor that could change your life? Make time for it before it becomes urgent.

**Y — Yearn for Less, Achieve More**
Simplify your schedule by reducing unnecessary commitments and focusing on meaningful pursuits. More isn't better — better is better. Tell the world what life you want by showing them how you spend your time.

**Z — Reach Your Zenith**
Strive for the highest level of success by meticulously organizing your time and actions. If you want to be the top spouse, top parent, top friend, top entrepreneur — do it with intention. The zenith is the version of yourself your Mount Everest goal requires you to become.
