# Zoning Workflow for Car Wash Development

This document covers how to help with zoning for a car wash site. There are two main tasks:
1. Drafting the initial planning department inquiry email
2. Filling out the standard city planning intake form (after getting a city response)

---

## Why Zoning Matters

Zoning is where most car wash deals die. A site can have perfect traffic, income, and lot size — and still be unbuildable if the entitlement path is too difficult or unclear.

Key facts about car wash zoning:
- Car washes are often classified as "Drive-in Facilities" in municipal codes
- Many commercial zones require conditional use permits (CUPs) for car washes
- Even former car wash sites may not be automatically re-approvable if the city's comprehensive plan has changed
- Industrial zoning is a red flag — rezoning from industrial is typically a long, risky process
- Environmental approvals can be extensive (water recycling, oil/water separation, discharge regulations)
- Multi-layer approvals are common: city, county, and sometimes state environmental
- Minimum 120-day inspection period is needed for zoning validation + engineering + environmental review + permit pre-approval

## Task 1: Draft the Planning Department Inquiry Email

### When to use this
When the user wants to contact the city/town planning department to confirm whether a car wash is allowed on a specific parcel.

### Rules for the email
- Keep it short and factual
- Do NOT request analysis, opinions, or feasibility conclusions from city staff
- Only ask for: (1) zoning status for car wash use, and (2) site plan submittal checklist / links
- Ask them to reply by email if at all possible (creates a paper trail)
- If the city only has a phone number, adapt it into a call script — same questions, same intent

### Email Template

Fill in the bracketed fields with what the user provides:

---

Subject: Car Wash Development Inquiry – [Property Address or Parcel Number]

Hello [Planning Department / Staff Name],

My name is [Your Name] with [Company Name]. We are evaluating a potential car wash development in [City/Town Name] and would like to confirm the City's site plan and entitlement requirements.

Could you please advise on:

1. Whether a car wash is **permitted or requires a conditional/special use approval** in the applicable zoning district, and

2. Where we can find the City's **site plan submittal checklist** or required drawings/documents for this use.

If there are existing online resources, ordinances, or application guides you recommend reviewing, a link would be greatly appreciated.

Thank you for your time and assistance.

Best regards,
[Your Name]
[Phone Number]
[Email]

---

### How to find the right contact
- Look for the City or Town **Planning Department** (not engineering or building initially)
- Use the general planning email unless a specific planner is listed
- City websites typically have this under "Community Development," "Planning & Zoning," or "Development Services"

## Task 2: Fill Out the Standard City Planning Intake Form

### When to use this
After the user has received a response from the city and needs to document it in the standard format before submitting for internal review.

### Instructions
- Extract only objective requirements from the city response
- Do not paraphrase or interpret — use direct quotes or exact language from city materials where possible
- A snapshot of the zoning use table or a direct quote from the city email is more useful than sending the full zoning code

## Zoning Risk Assessment

After filling out the intake form or reviewing zoning info, help the user assess entitlement risk:

| Zoning Status | Risk Level | Typical Timeline | Recommendation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Permitted by right | Low | 3–6 months for permits | Move forward — best case |
| Conditional use permit required | Medium | 3–9 months | Acceptable — confirm scope of hearing process |
| Special use permit | Medium-High | 6–12 months | Proceed with caution — factor timeline into LOI |
| Rezoning required | High | 6–18+ months | Only pursue if site is exceptional |
| Prohibited / no path | Deal-killer | — | Reject |
| Unknown / unclear | High | Unknown | Do not advance until confirmed |

**Key warning**: "Unknown zoning with no clarity" is an immediate flag. The city response must confirm use status before a deal can be treated as viable.

## Common Zoning Pitfalls

- Assuming a former car wash site is automatically approvable — city plans change
- Confusing "commercial" zoning with car wash permission — not the same
- Industrial zoning that "sort of" works — rarely does without rezoning
- Ignoring stormwater and retention requirements that can eat into usable land
- Missing setback requirements that can reduce the effective lot area significantly
- Not asking about signage restrictions — monument signs are often critical for visibility
