# Welcome to Your Iris Vault

This is your second brain. It's a folder of plain markdown files that you
own forever — even if you stop using IRIS, even if Claude disappears, even
if all the AI models change tomorrow. Markdown is forever.

## What lives here

- **Identity files** (`me.md`, `my-everest.md`, `my-voice.md`, `my-business.md`) — who you are, what you're building, what matters. Edit these directly.
- **`index.md`** — navigation file. IRIS reads this first to find her way around.
- **`Calendar/`** — daily notes. One file per day.
- **`Efforts/`** — active projects.
- **`Atlas/`** — reference material, saved articles, research.
- **`Concepts/`** — synthesized insights over time.
- **`maps/`** — vault structure guide + your instructions to IRIS.

## How IRIS uses this vault

- She reads your identity files before any important conversation.
- She reads `maps/iris-rules.md` to know how you want her to behave.
- She appends check-ins under reserved sections in your daily notes (she never edits your own writing).
- In later phases, she'll propose new insights that you can approve or reject.

## Your rules

1. **You own everything here.** Edit, move, delete — it's your vault.
2. **IRIS never touches your content.** She only appends to clearly marked reserved sections.
3. **Nothing leaves this folder** unless you choose to sync it (iCloud, Dropbox, Obsidian Sync, or nothing).
4. **If you stop using IRIS, your vault still works** in any markdown editor.

## Getting started

1. Open [me.md](me.md) and replace the guidance prompts with your real answers.
2. Do the same for [my-everest.md](my-everest.md), [my-business.md](my-business.md), and [my-voice.md](my-voice.md).
3. Check [maps/iris-rules.md](maps/iris-rules.md) — that's where you tell IRIS how you want her to show up.
4. Come back to [index.md](index.md) anytime to see your vault's structure.

Take your time. The vault gets better the more you feed it.
