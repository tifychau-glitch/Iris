# Coaching Questions Reference

Questions organized by excavation area. These are options, not a script. Choose based on where the conversation is and what the person needs. Read the room. Don't ask questions you already know the answer to.

---

## 1. Clarity and Conviction Check

Goal: Determine whether this goal is truly theirs — or an inherited expectation, industry benchmark, or idea they've never really owned.

- "Say that goal out loud like you mean it. Go."
- "Whose definition of success is that?"
- "If nobody was watching — no audience, no income pressure, no family expectations — what would the goal be?"
- "You said 'I think.' Do you know, or do you think?"
- "What would you have to give up to go all in on that? And is that okay with you?"
- "What happens if you don't achieve it? How do you feel about that?"

**Signals:** Hedging language ("probably," "I guess," "maybe"), goals that sound like elevator pitches rather than personal conviction, inability to answer quickly and specifically.

---

## 2. Identity Work

Goal: Uncover who they need to become — not just what they need to do. This is the identity gap.

- "Who is the version of you that's already achieved this? Describe that person."
- "What does that person do on a Tuesday morning that you currently don't?"
- "What do you need to stop being to get there?"
- "What belief about yourself would have to change for this goal to feel inevitable?"
- "The version of you building a $10M business — what does that person say no to that you currently say yes to?"
- "What identity are you protecting right now that's keeping you where you are?"

**Note:** This is often the most uncomfortable area. People are willing to talk about what they need to do. They resist talking about who they need to stop being. Stay with it.

---

## 3. Reverse Engineering the Goal

Goal: Break the big goal into milestones that reveal whether the timeline is honest.

- "What needs to be true 12 months from now for the 3-year goal to still be on track?"
- "What needs to happen in the next 90 days for the 12-month target to be reachable?"
- "What has to happen this month for the 90-day goal to be possible?"
- "What's your current trajectory producing? And what does the goal require?"
- "If you kept doing exactly what you're doing now, where does that land you in 3 years?"

**Note:** If the gap between current trajectory and goal is large, name it plainly and move on. Don't soften it. The gap is data.

---

## 4. Honest Gap Analysis

Goal: Name where they are and where they need to be — without minimizing the gap or catastrophizing it.

- "Where are you right now, honestly? Revenue, momentum, team, capacity — what's the real picture?"
- "What does achieving this goal require that you don't currently have?"
- "Are you closer to the start or the middle of this climb?"
- "What are you pretending isn't a problem right now?"
- "If a trusted advisor looked at your current situation and your goal, what would they say?"

**Note:** Most people need permission to say the honest number. Make it safe to do so. The goal is to get real data, not to make them feel bad.

---

## 5. Ceiling Identification

Goal: Identify the single biggest obstacle — the thing that, if removed, would change the most.

- "What's the one thing standing between you and this goal right now?"
- "What are you avoiding that you know you need to address?"
- "If you knew you couldn't fail, what would you do differently tomorrow?"
- "Is the ceiling a skill gap, a relationship gap, a decision you haven't made, or a belief about yourself?"
- "You're working hard. What are you not working on?"
- "What decision have you been putting off that you know needs to be made?"

**Note:** Usually one thing. When people list five ceilings, ask them to pick the one that unlocks the others. There's almost always a primary constraint.

---

## 6. The Why Underneath the Why

Goal: Get past the surface answer (money, success, status) to the real driver (freedom, security, validation, legacy, love).

- "What does achieving this goal actually give you?"
- "And underneath that — what does that give you?"
- "If you had that, what would be different about your daily life?"
- "Who are you doing this for?"
- "What are you trying to prove, and to whom?"
- "What would you finally be able to stop worrying about?"

**Note:** Keep going until you hit the emotional core. The surface answer is rarely the real answer. The real why is the fuel that doesn't run out.

**Watch for conflicts:** If their real why is freedom but their goal requires building a 50-person organization, that tension needs to be named. Either the goal evolves or the sacrifice gets consciously accepted.

---

## 7. Values Alignment

Goal: Identify whether the goal as currently defined fits with what they actually value — and name any real tensions.

- "What matters most to you in your life right now? Not your work — your life."
- "Does this goal require you to sacrifice something you said you'd never sacrifice?"
- "If you achieved this goal exactly as you've defined it — would you be happy?"
- "What does the path to this goal look like day-to-day? Is that a life you actually want?"
- "Is there a version of this goal that would let you have both the achievement and the thing you're afraid of losing?"

**Note:** Don't let them avoid the tension. If the conflict is real, name it. They can consciously accept the sacrifice or redefine the goal — but they can't unknowingly live in the contradiction.

---

## 8. The "Already There" Visualization

Goal: Get specific, sensory detail about what life looks like when the goal is achieved. Vague goals produce vague motivation.

- "Describe a Tuesday morning when you've achieved this goal. Where are you? Who's around you? What are you doing?"
- "What are you no longer dealing with that you deal with now?"
- "What are you finally able to do that you can't do today?"
- "What does your calendar look like? How do you spend your time?"
- "Who's in your life that isn't there now? Who's no longer in it?"
- "What did achieving this cost you — and do you think it was worth it?"

**Note:** The visualization often surfaces hidden requirements. "Oh, if that's what I want, then I also need..." This is useful data. Follow it.

## Pace Morby Framework Questions

From Pace Morby's goal clarity training — useful for surfacing resistance and building accountability.

**Surface the real goal:**
- "What would you attempt if you knew you could not fail?"
- "What's the version of success that scares you a little?"

**Force specificity:**
- "Give me a number. By when?"
- "What does success look like in a way that's measurable?"

**Force consequence:**
- "What happens if you don't achieve this in 3 years? What's the cost?"
- "Who else is affected if you don't follow through?"

**Expose the work:**
- "What would you have to do every week for the next year to make this real?"
- "What are you not willing to do that the goal actually requires?"

**Name the avoidance:**
- "What do you keep doing instead of the thing that would move you forward?"
- "What have you been telling yourself about why it's not the right time?"

**Close with commitment:**
- "What are you committing to this week? Say it like you mean it."
- "Who's going to hold you accountable, and when will you report back?"

## FATE Framework (Pace Morby)

When a session needs more structure, use FATE:

- **F — Focus**: What is the one goal? What does it look like when achieved?
- **A — Authority**: Who is the version of you that achieves this? What authority do they carry?
- **T — Tribe**: Who needs to be in your life? Who needs to not be?
- **E — Emotion**: What is the emotional core? What does this goal actually give you?

---

## Cadence Check-In Prompts

For Reconnect Mode or mid-session temperature checks:

**Daily:**
> "Did you take at least one action today that moved you toward the goal?"

**Weekly:**
> "What was your biggest win this week toward the goal? What did you avoid?"

**Monthly:**
> "Are you closer to the 12-month milestone than you were 30 days ago? By how much?"

**When stuck:**
> "You've been stuck for [X time]. What's the real reason you haven't moved?"
