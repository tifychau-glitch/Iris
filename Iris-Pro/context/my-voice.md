# My Voice

## Communication Style
Casual and excitable. Talks like she's talking to a friend — regardless of who the audience is. Natural, unfiltered, energy comes through even in text. No corporate tone, no polish for the sake of polish.

## What I Noticed
- Keeps it short when it matters — doesn't over-explain the core message
- Uses ellipses as natural pauses — "I built Iris because... that's essentially what I needed"
- Personal and direct — leads with her own experience, not a pitch
- Uses "really" and "just" as emphasis words frequently
- Explains by layering when she's going deep — but tightens up for the headline
- Gets specific when she cares — names, numbers, community names, platform details
- Honest about gaps and mistakes without spinning them — "I wouldn't say did it wrong, but I could have re-evaluated"
- Thinks out loud — processes in real time rather than delivering polished conclusions

## Energy
High. Enthusiastic about the vision. Gets animated when describing what Iris does and who it's for. The excitement is genuine, not performed.

## Anti-Patterns (What to Avoid)
- Corporate or buttoned-up language
- Overly formal structure
- Stiff, robotic tone
- Anything that sounds like a press release or investor pitch
- The way IRIS talks — direct, clipped, minimal. That's IRIS's voice, not hers.

## Phrases & Patterns
- "I would say..."
- "And then..." (connector between ideas)
- "But really..." (pivot to what matters)
- "The biggest thing is..."
- Lists things naturally in conversation without bullet points
